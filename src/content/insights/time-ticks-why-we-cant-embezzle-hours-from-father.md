As we stand on the brink of another year-end, I find myself musing humorously over a question that's more philosophical than financial: Why do we move forwards in time and not backwards? After all, in the world of finance, we're quite adept at juggling past, present, and future numbers. Yet, when it comes to time, it's a one-way street. This is an attempt on a light-hearted exploration of this temporal conundrum, with a CFO's twist.

### The Unyielding Ledger of Time

Imagine if time were like a company's ledger. We could accrue hours, carry forward leisure moments to busy days, or even better, amortize a particularly enjoyable experience over several dreary weeks! Alas, time, the most uncooperative accountant, insists on a chronological sequence, refusing to entertain our creative accounting with hours and minutes.

### Fiscal Year-End vs. Time’s Year-End

In finance, the year-end is a time for closing books, tallying scores, and starting anew with a clean slate. Time, however, seems to follow a different set of GAAP (Generally Accepted Aging Principles). It rolls forward relentlessly, adding to our personal balance sheets of life experiences, with no option to 'close the year' and start afresh, despite our pleas at New Year’s resolutions.

### Depreciation of Moments

We often talk about asset depreciation, but time depreciates moments in a blink. The 'value' of a moment diminishes as soon as it passes, becoming a memory, an intangible asset on our mental balance sheets. If only we could apply straight-line depreciation to our favorite moments, extending their value over a longer period!

### Time: The Market That Never Crashes

In the financial world, markets fluctuate, stocks rise and fall, but time? It’s the bull market that never crashes. It consistently moves forward, impervious to economic downturns, pandemics, or even our most fervent wishes for a pause during a particularly relaxing vacation.

### Quarterly Forecasts vs. Life’s Uncertainty

As CFOs, we are accustomed to making quarterly forecasts, predicting revenues and expenses. Time, however, laughs in the face of forecasts. It brings unforeseen twists and turns, making our life’s journey more unpredictable than the most volatile stock market.

### The Time Budget Deficit

In finance, budgeting is key. We allocate funds judiciously, striving for a surplus. In life, however, we’re always in a time budget deficit. No matter how efficiently we plan, 24 hours never seem enough, especially when the audit season rolls around!

### Embracing the Forward March

As we approach the year-end, let’s embrace time’s forward march with humor and grace. While we can’t backtrack or slow its pace, we can choose to invest our time wisely, ensuring rich dividends of joy, experiences, and learning. Here’s to another year where we balance our sheets, close our books, and march forward in time, one tick tock at a time. Happy New Year to all my fellow time-travelers. Let’s make every moment count (until time invents a backward button, that is).
