Being a CFO in fintech sounds cool—until it’s 2:47am and you're wide awake, wondering if your fraud controls holds, your Series C runway will stretch, or your compliance stack is six months behind where it should be.

Fintech moves fast. And finance leaders in fintech? We’re expected to move faster, while still playing risk cop, growth architect, and regulator whisperer all at once.

Here are the five real things keeping fintech CFOs up at night in 2025. If you’re in the trenches, I bet at least some of these will feel painfully familiar.

* * *

### 1\. Fraud and cyber threats: The flip side of innovation

In fintech, we’re building beautiful digital experiences. So are the fraudsters. Every new feature, payment rail, wallet, or account layer is a potential vulnerability. And the attackers are no longer script kiddies—they’re organized, well-funded, and relentless.

I worry less about big, obvious hacks and more about subtle, ongoing manipulation—synthetic identities, API misuse, deepfake onboarding. Every day we scale, the surface area expands.

No fintech grows faster than trust can be lost. And as CFO, I know that one breach doesn’t just hit the P&L—it hits valuation, investor confidence, and customer stickiness.

* * *

### 2\. AI, automation… and the risk of speed without control

We all want faster reconciliation, smarter risk decisions, real-time anomaly detection. AI promises all of that—and more. But every tool we add increases complexity. It also introduces new risks: explainability gaps, regulatory grey zones, and dependence on black-box models.

As a fintech CFO, the pressure to automate is real. But so is the pressure to understand what’s happening under the hood.

My 2am thought? “We’ve sped up every decision in the business. Are we sure we’ve made them better?”

* * *

### 3\. Regulatory drag meets rocket-fuel growth

No sector attracts more regulatory attention than fintech. Every product we launch sits at the intersection of financial conduct, data privacy, AML, and (increasingly) ESG. And the rules are still catching up with the tech.

Licensing, audits, reporting obligations—it’s a full-time job just staying compliant in one jurisdiction. Add cross-border operations? Welcome to the funhouse.

What keeps me up? That we’re so busy building the future, we forget the cost of a missed filing, a sloppy control, or a regulator we didn’t see coming.

* * *

### 4\. Liquidity and runway: more than just burn rate

Yes, burn rate matters. But in fintech, it’s not just how fast you’re spending—it’s how fast capital markets shift. One quarter you’re the darling of VCs. The next, sentiment changes, and you’re suddenly in cash conservation mode.

Add in interest rate volatility, token market disruption (for crypto players), or bank partnership risk—and cash planning becomes a daily exercise in scenario modelling.

CFOs in fintech don’t just track the runway. We obsess over weather conditions on the landing strip.

* * *

### 5\. Talent: can we grow without breaking culture (or people)?

Fintech attracts brilliant, ambitious people. But it also burns them out. High expectations, hybrid models, and the constant pressure to “scale now and fix later” can wear teams down.

I lie awake wondering: Are we building a finance team that’s fit for what’s next—or just surviving the sprint? Can we keep top talent engaged, learning, and aligned in a world where roles change every six months?

Culture isn’t soft stuff. In fintech, it’s survival infrastructure.

* * *

### The real job: see around corners without losing focus

The truth? I love this job. I wouldn’t trade the chaos, complexity, and creativity of fintech for anything.

But let’s stop pretending the CFO role is just about budgets and board decks. We’re operators. Strategists. Risk managers. Culture setters. And yes—sleepless, because we care about getting it right.

If you’re a fintech founder, give your CFO room to think beyond the monthly close. If you’re an investor, ask better questions than just “What’s runway?” And if you’re a fellow CFO—let’s swap notes. We’re all figuring this out together.
